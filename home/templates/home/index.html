Illegal Slavery Survivor:

Miguel

Miguel, enslaved in Florida's orange groves, endured threats and exploitation after seeking work to pay for his son's cancer treatment. The Coalition of Immokalee Workers discovered his situation and orchestrated his rescue. Now free, Miguel works on his own terms, enjoys time off, and sends money to his family. Most importantly, his son has recovered and is healthy.

Sweatshop Worker:

Flor Molina

Flor Molina was trafficked from Mexico to a garment factory in Los Angeles, where she faced abuse and 18-hour workdays. After pleading to attend church, she escaped and cooperated with federal authorities investigating the factory. Flor was granted a T visa to remain in the U.S. She has since become an advocate for trafficking survivors, using her voice to represent those who cannot speak for themselves.

Trafficking Victim:

Evelyn Chumbow

Sold by her uncle in Cameroon, Evelyn was trafficked to Maryland as a child and forced into domestic servitude. She endured beatings, starvation, and constant degradation. At 17, Evelyn ran away and found help through a priest who contacted the police. Today, Evelyn is a survivor activist and board member of The Human Trafficking Legal Center, working to combat modern slavery and sharing her story to raise awareness and inspire change.
